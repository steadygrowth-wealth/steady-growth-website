Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some of the most commonly asked questions related to mutual funds, investing, documentation, regulations, taxation, and onboarding. If you still have questions, feel free to contact me anytime.

Getting Started

1. What exactly does a Mutual Fund Distributor do?

A Mutual Fund Distributor helps investors select suitable mutual fund schemes by understanding their goals, risk profile, and investment timeline. They also assist with onboarding, paperwork, KYC, transaction execution, portfolio review & continued guidance without providing stock tips or guaranteed returns.

2. Who can invest in mutual funds?

Almost anyone—salaried professionals, entrepreneurs, homemakers, students above 18, NRIs, HUFs, trusts and companies can invest, provided they meet KYC and compliance norms. Minors can also invest under guardian mode.

3. Do I need a demat account to invest?

No, a demat account is not mandatory. Units can be held in statement mode through NJ Wealth or respective AMCs. However, investors may choose demat mode if they prefer all assets consolidated under one demat account.

4. Can I start investing with small amounts?

Yes. Systematic Investment Plans (SIPs) can start from as low as ₹500 per month in many mutual fund schemes, making it suitable for first-time and young investors.

5. I’m completely new to investing. Is this suitable for beginners?

Absolutely. Mutual funds are professionally managed and designed for long-term wealth creation. Beginners often find them simpler than trading or direct equity, as risks are diversified and decisions are guided by SEBI-regulated fund managers.

6. Can I pause or stop my SIP anytime?

Yes, SIPs are flexible. You can modify, pause or stop them whenever required without penalty, subject to AMC terms.

7. Will mutual fund investments protect me from market losses?

No investment offering market-linked returns is risk-free. However, proper asset allocation, diversification, and long-term holding greatly reduce volatility-related risks.

8. How long should I stay invested?

There is no fixed duration. Ideally, equity investments require a 5–10+ year horizon for meaningful wealth creation, whereas debt funds may suit short-term goals.

9. Can I invest jointly with a family member?

Yes, joint holding is allowed with up to three holders based on nomination, mode, and compliance rules.

10. Will you help me decide which scheme to invest in?

Yes. Scheme suitability is assessed after analyzing your goals, risk level, horizon, liquidity requirements and existing portfolio to avoid overlap and excessive risk.

Process & Documents

1. What documents are required to start investing?

PAN, Aadhar, passport-size photo, bank proof (cheque/cancelled cheque) and signature image. Additional documents apply for NRIs, corporates, trusts and minors.

2. What is CKYC and why is it mandatory?

Central KYC is a government-regulated identity verification system that eliminates repeated KYC submissions across financial institutions. It ensures compliance & prevents identity misuse.

3. How long does the onboarding process take?

If all documents are correct, most investors are onboarded digitally within 10–30 minutes. Manual verification may take up to 24–48 hours.

4. Can I invest fully online?

Yes. From KYC to SIP setup, every step is paperless and can be completed via mobile, laptop or tablet through the NJ Wealth platform.

5. How are transactions tracked?

All transactions are reflected in NJ Wealth as well as AMC portals. You also receive email + SMS confirmations and consolidated account statements.

6. Can I start SIP from multiple bank accounts?

Yes, you may register multiple bank mandates. SIPs can be linked to specific bank accounts depending on your preference.

7. Can I transfer my existing MF portfolio to your advice?

Yes, if they are in regular plans, units can be transferred under ARN change request. Direct-plan units cannot be converted to regular schemes.

8. What if my CKYC is rejected or pending?

We assist you with corrections such as signature mismatch, address update, name change etc. Re-KYC may be required if old KYC is non-compliant.

9. Can minors invest?

Yes, via a guardian until age 18. When attaining majority, KYC & bank change is required.

10. How will I receive reports?

Portfolio statements, capital gains reports, SIP transaction logs and goal-reports are shared periodically or on request.

Risk & Returns

1. Are mutual funds safe?

There are low-risk, medium-risk and high-risk funds. Safety depends on fund category, market conditions and holding period, not just brand or AMC size.

2. What returns should I expect?

No guaranteed returns. Historically, equity funds have delivered 10–15% CAGR over long periods, while debt and hybrid funds deliver lower but stable returns.

3. Why does NAV fluctuate?

NAV reflects the real-time market value of the fund’s underlying securities such as stocks, bonds and cash holdings — hence it moves daily.

4. What if the market crashes?

A market fall is temporary in most economic cycles. The key is portfolio allocation and discipline, not timing. Down cycles often become favourable entry points.

5. Can I lose money permanently?

Permanent loss generally happens only if you exit in panic or choose unsuitable schemes. Staying invested long term with proper allocation reduces such risks.

6. Are mutual funds better than FDs?

FDs offer capital safety and fixed return, while mutual funds offer potentially higher inflation-beating returns with market risk. Most investors need a mix, not either/or.

7. Should I withdraw profits regularly?

Profit booking too frequently may affect long-term compounding. Withdraw only if goal-based or based on rebalancing decisions.

8. Which funds are safest?

Ultra-short, liquid and certain debt funds have low volatility, but even they are not 100% risk-free. Category choice must match goal timeline.

9. What is exit load?

A small fee charged if you withdraw before the minimum holding period as defined by the scheme. It encourages investment discipline.

10. How do I control risk?

Diversification, appropriate allocation, review frequency, long holding period, and avoiding emotional decisions.

NRI & Overseas Investors

1. Can NRIs invest in Indian mutual funds?

Yes, NRIs can invest from most countries subject to FEMA & FATCA rules. Some AMCs may restrict investments from the USA/Canada.

2. Is NRE or NRO account required?

Yes. For NRIs, investments must be routed via NRE or NRO bank accounts depending on repatriation status.

3. What is FATCA?

Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act is mandatory for global tax reporting and must be submitted digitally before transactions.

4. How does taxation work for NRIs?

Capital gains tax applies based on holding period and fund category. TDS is deducted at source as per Indian tax laws.

5. Can NRIs repatriate funds?

Yes, if invested from NRE accounts and subject to FEMA guidelines. For NRO accounts, repatriation is capped annually.

6. Can NRIs invest jointly?

Yes, subject to KYC, residency proof and bank account status of all holders.

7. Can NRIs continue investing after returning to India?

Yes. Re-KYC with updated resident status and domestic bank account needs to be updated.

8. Do NRIs need to come to India for KYC?

No, digital onboarding is allowed via video-KYC in many cases.

9. Can NRIs invest using foreign currency?

No, funds must be routed through Indian NRE/NRO accounts in INR.

10. Are there restrictions for US/Canada-based NRIs?

Some AMCs allow investments with extra compliance; others restrict. Allowed fund list will be shared case-to-case.

Fees & Plans

1. Do I have to pay you advisory fees?

No separate advisory fee is charged. The distributor earns commission from the AMCs under regular plan model as per SEBI guidelines.

2. What is the difference between Direct & Regular plans?

Direct plans have slightly lower expense ratio but require self-managed decisions. Regular plans include distributor-supported services, monitoring and guidance.

3. Is there any hidden cost?

No. Expense ratio is disclosed by AMCs. Commission rates are transparent and uniform as per SEBI norms.

4. Do I have to pay platform charges?

No, onboarding and transactions via NJ Wealth are free for investors.

5. Will you assist in reviewing & rebalancing without extra cost?

Yes. Portfolio review is a key part of long-term wealth management and is included.

6. What is the exit load?

Exit load varies by scheme category, usually applicable within 6–12 months for equity schemes. Details are shown before completing transactions.

7. How do SIP cancellation charges work?

There are no penalty charges for cancellation. However, bank mandates remain active until revoked.

8. What happens if I miss SIP due to insufficient balance?

It will retry or skip depending on mode. Multiple failures may cancel the SIP automatically.

9. Do you guarantee profits if I invest for many years?

No. While long durations improve outcome probability, returns depend on market performance, fund category and discipline.

10. Which plan should I choose—Direct or Regular?

If you can research and manage your portfolio independently, direct plans are suitable. If you prefer personalised guidance, risk alignment and review support, regular plans offer value.
